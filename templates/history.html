History

St. Anne’s First Grade College for Women, located on Miller Road, Bangalore, was established with the mission of nurturing the minds and spirits of young women through education. It is a proud initiative of the Religious Society of the Sisters of St. Anne, Bangalore, a congregation rooted in faith and social upliftment.

The society was founded under the visionary guidance of Rt. Rev. Bishop Stephen Louis Charbonnaux, whose dream was to empower women by providing them with holistic, value-based education. With its headquarters at #2 Miller Road, Bangalore, the congregation has grown extensively, establishing educational and social institutions across India, Germany, and Africa.

Since its inception, the college has stood as a beacon of academic excellence, moral integrity, and dedicated service — preparing generations of women to become responsible citizens and compassionate leaders.

Over the years, our college has embraced innovation in education, integrating modern pedagogy with strong traditional values. Our alumnae continue to make their mark globally — in science, literature, business, civil services, and social reform.

Our Founder

Rt. Rev. Bishop Stephen Louis Charbonnaux

Rt. Rev. Bishop Stephen Louis Charbonnaux, a missionary of the Paris Foreign Missions Society and the first Vicar Apostolic of Mysore, was a profound scholar and visionary leader. Deeply committed to evangelization and social development, he founded the Congregation of the Sisters of St. Anne in Bangalore with the aim of serving women through education and empowerment.

Fluent in multiple South Indian languages, Bishop Charbonnaux understood the cultural pulse of the region and worked tirelessly to uplift marginalized communities. His legacy continues to guide the mission of our college — to educate, inspire, and transform.

Inspired by Christ, the eternal teacher, and by St. Anne, the teacher of the Blessed Virgin Mary, our founder emphasized the values of wisdom, compassion, and service. Today, his vision lives on in every classroom, every lesson, and every woman shaped by St. Anne’s.
